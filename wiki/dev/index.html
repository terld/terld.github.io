Devs Unite!

Great! So you want to help code Terld, well, here's how to get started:

  • first, tune in to terld on github
  • next, fork our repo
  • third, make the modifications that you want (there might be some cool things to fix on the issues page.)
  • last, add a pull request to the original repo comparing it to your forked version


that's it! Now you can sit back and rest assured that we will take a look at your changes!

 

Help Make The Future

you might be thinking, "oh, well this is all good fun but where can i really start to shape the future?", the answer is the terld sandbox. The terld sandbox is where we keep future versions of terld so that you can help craft them, in fact, that is where this wiki is hosted, so you can help write documentation for us and spread the word. The terld sandbox works the same way as the current version of terld, you fork it, fix it, pull it, and love it. You can find the sandbox here.

I can't code, But I'd Love to Help!

that's ok. Some of our greatest technologies come from people who can't neccessarly program, but say "can we do this!?", and usually the answer is yes.

here are a few ways that you can get involved:

  • have a suggestion? send us your idea
  • you can do QA (Quality Assurance Testing). simply put, having well made products is of the upmost importance to terld, and you would help test them for us, all you need is a GitHub account, computer with internet access, and an eye for detail. Now when something on terld is not working correctly, just submit it to the issue tracker and include some additional information (e.g. what the issue was, your browser, etc.)
  • spread the word, just write or say something nice about us, share our site with your friends (or enemies [or even frenemies]) and help us become the next big huge thing.
  • write stuff. you can write awesome help articles for the user-generated wiki. Just write and post, no need to fork, pull, or push. Because they don't have to be approved by us first, they go live when you want them to.*

Quality Assurance

Quality is very important to terld, so we have some basic testing Guidelines.

your browser

use whatever browser you want, a diverse testing environment helps us ensure that terld is as good as ever. If you are feeling really helpful, you can download some more browsers and test on them, too.

general testing

make sure to examine every aspect of of terld, even what might seem like the most minor details, this helps us create a better terld, and helps us improve our support.

mobile phones

feel free to test on you smart phone, too. Mobile is an ever growing platform that is very important, because as it grows, so will the amount of mobile users, and with more users come more site visits on the go, so if terld is only accessible in a pinching, panning, tap-to-zoom sort of way, we would love to know.

submitting a bug

you can submit a bug to the issues page. We would appreciate it if you let us know what the bug is, what you were doing when you found it, how you found it, what browser you were using, and what OS you were on.

Marketing

The rules behind marketing are simple, add something about terld to your site with a link back to us. Although we can't offer you any money or prizes, you'll get that warm, fuzzy feeling in your heart because deep down, you know that you did something great today!




still not sure what's going on? well hang tight, we're working on making more developer documentation to help you out.


* when writing articles on the wiki, please do not use any language that may be perceived as offensive. We don't want to have to ask you to remove it. Please start terld off happy and family friendly. :)
